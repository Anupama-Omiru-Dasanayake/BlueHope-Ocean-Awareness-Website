Our Mission

Our mission is to inspire and empower students to protect marine ecosystems by raising awareness about ocean conservation, encouraging sustainable habits, and promoting everyday actions that help reduce marine pollution and preserve life below water for future generations.

Why Oceans Matter ?

Biodiversity

The ocean is home to millions of species, from tiny plankton to the largest animals on Earth. Coral reefs, seagrass beds, and deep-sea ecosystems support a vast range of marine life and help maintain ecological balance.

Climate Regulation

Oceans absorb a large portion of the carbon dioxide produced by human activities and help regulate global temperatures. Ocean currents distribute heat around the planet, influencing weather patterns and stabilizing Earth's climate.

Oxygen Production

Marine plants and microscopic organisms such as phytoplankton produce over half of the oxygen we breathe. Healthy oceans are therefore essential for sustaining life on Earth.

Food and Livelihoods

Millions of people depend on oceans for food, employment, and economic activities such as fishing and tourism. Sustainable ocean management helps ensure that these resources remain available for future generations.

Ocean Facts

71%

Earth covered by oceans

The ocean covers about 71% of the Earth's surface, making it the largest ecosystem on our planet. These vast waters regulate global climate, support marine biodiversity, and play a vital role in sustaining life on Earth.

50%

Oxygen produced by oceans

More than half of the oxygen we breathe is produced by the ocean. Tiny marine organisms called phytoplankton perform photosynthesis and release oxygen into the atmosphere, making healthy oceans essential for life.

25%

Marine species depend on coral reefs

Although coral reefs cover less than 1% of the ocean floor, they support around 25% of all marine species. These vibrant ecosystems provide food, shelter, and breeding grounds for countless marine animals.

Threats to Marine Life

Plastic Pollution

Plastic waste is one of the biggest threats to ocean ecosystems. Millions of tons of plastic enter the oceans every year, harming marine animals and breaking down into tiny particles that pollute marine environments.

Overfishing

Overfishing occurs when fish are caught faster than they can reproduce. Unsustainable fishing practices reduce fish populations and disrupt the balance of marine ecosystems.

Coral Reefs Loss

Coral reefs support thousands of marine species and are essential for healthy ocean ecosystems. However, climate change, pollution, and destructive fishing practices are threatening these fragile habitats.
